On-site interviews: What to expect

We’re excited you’re coming to interview at Gojek! Your visit will give you an opportunity to meet some more of our team and get a feel for what it’s like to work here. We’ve put together this short guide to give you an idea of what your day will involve. Like many things at a fast-growing startup, we’re constantly improving and iterating on how we interview, so if you have any comments, concerns, or questions, please let us know. We look forward to seeing you soon.

Some general tips

Many of the interviews included during your on-site visit are designed to help us understand how you might approach common scenarios encountered at Gojek. We’ve designed our case studies to reflect the types of problems you may have to solve, but the projects and topics are fictional. This is to ensure that we evaluate you based on your Program Manager skill-set and not based on your knowledge of Gojek. You’ll always have the opportunity to ask us questions about anything you don’t understand.

Program management at Gojek is cross-functional by nature. To that end, we’ve designed an interview loop that will allow you to meet with various teams including Program Management, and cross-functional stakeholders.

Prior to your visit, we will provide you with a tentative schedule, including interview times and interviewer details (name and team/role).

HOW TO PREPARE

    • Take a look at our blog.
    • Read through our product services at www.go-jek.com so that you have a basic understanding of what each of our products do.
    • Read about Gojek’s mission and our three pillars.
    • Get your whiteboard marker ready! We’ll have a few discussions where you work through problems with the interviewer on the whiteboard; for these, the intent is to evaluate how you solve problems and collaborate with teammates, rather than your Gojek expertise.
    • Consider what's important to you in a job, what you'd like to see in a work environment, how you partner with colleagues and managers, and what you'd like to avoid. Feel free to ask about company goals, team organization, Gojek’s values, etc
    • Take some time to review the job description and think of how your past experience relates to this role. You might find it useful to come up with a few flexible examples of projects you've worked on in the past. Examples of past experience and work often come up during interview conversations, and often it's easiest to draw upon a few vetted examples instead of coming up with something on the spot! (Please don’t discuss anything that your previous or current employers would consider confidential).
    • Be well-rested and ready to think on your feet, as some of the interviews will require you to problem-solve in real time.
What we evaluate

We want you to have the opportunity to present your best self. If you’re considering what to emphasize, these are our top five priorities:

  • Leadership
    • Are you able to provide oversight & guidance to avoid slippage, build a strong pulse & feedback loop on organizational needs, and lead the planning, execution & budgeting of strategic initiatives in partnership with department heads?
  • Execution
    • Are you able to set project priorities and sequencing, milestone planning, resource allocation, and hiring/talent needs to execute against business area objectives? Are you able to encourage team members to deliver on their commitments and ensure corrective measures are taken on time to bring the project back on track?
  • Communication
    • Are you able to address questions and concerns from stakeholders? Effectively communicate and roll out complex, high-impact programs that impact a large number of stakeholders e.g. performance management? Are you able to streamline communication channels for easy & effective information sharing?
  • Development
    • Are you able to implement an effective system of project status tracking and coordinate & set up focus groups for feedback. Are you able to share the successful practices, frameworks, tools & lessons learned by your team back to the larger company?
  • Exploration
    • Are you able to audit process effectiveness through qualitative & quantitative means and compare our processes against the market? Are you able to identify opportunities for business growth or identify opportunities for more PgM work?
How long will the interview take?

While some candidates may have a shorter day with us, it’s best to plan to be at Gojek for about five hours. If you have any time constraints, please be sure to let your recruiter know.

Wondering what to wear?

Many people at Gojek show up to work in jeans and a t-shirt (or button down/blouse). We recommend simply wearing what you're most accustomed to wearing, because that’s what we do!

Additional notes and accommodations

Breaks:

    • We will do our best to provide a 15 minute break and/or lunch/coffee break during your time on site, especially if you'll be here for more than 3 hours. That said, we are happy to accommodate additional breaks or fewer breaks, depending on your needs and preferences, just let us know!
    • To recent mothers, we are happy to accommodate any breaks needed during your on-site visit. Please be sure to inform your recruiting partner, so we can make the arrangements that are best for you!

Accommodations:

    • If you need any accommodations to make your interview process a success, please don’t hesitate to ask.

Thank you again for taking the time to come in to Gojek. We want to make your experience as pleasant as possible, so please let your recruiter know if there is anything else we can do to set you up for success. Our team is very excited to meet you!
